About the Municipality

Where the Cool
Highlands Meet
Warm Hospitality

Impasug-ong is a landlocked municipality in the province of Bukidnon, located in Northern Mindanao, Philippines. Sitting at an elevation above 1,000 meters above sea level, it is known for its cool climate, vast pineapple plantations, and the indigenous Talaandig people.

Discover

Tourist Attractions

From misty mountains to cultural landmarks — Impasug-ong offers experiences unlike anywhere else in Mindanao.

🍍
Agriculture Tourism

Del Monte Pineapple Plantation

One of the largest pineapple plantations in Asia, the iconic Del Monte farms stretch across the highlands of Impasug-ong, offering guided plantation tours.

🌊
Natural Attraction

Pulangui River

The Pulangui (Rio Grande de Mindanao) flows through the municipality, offering scenic views, fishing, and riverbank recreational activities for visitors.

🎭
Cultural Heritage

Talaandig Cultural Village

Immerse yourself in the vibrant indigenous culture of the Talaandig people — known for their colorful artwork, rituals, music, and traditional crafts.

🏔️
Ecotourism

Mt. Kitanglad Range

Part of the Mt. Kitanglad Natural Park — a UNESCO Man and Biosphere Reserve, home to rare flora & fauna including the Philippine Eagle habitat.

🌿
Eco-Park

Sungco Eco-Park

A serene nature park featuring gardens, walking trails, and lush highland landscapes perfect for family outings and nature photography.

⛺
Adventure

Camp Phillips

A historic highland camp that now serves as a gateway community to the pineapple plantations, offering a cool climate retreat and local market experiences.

Heritage & Traditions

Our Culture & People

Impasug-ong is home to the Talaandig — one of Mindanao's most culturally rich indigenous peoples, known for their art, music, and spiritual traditions.

🎨

Talaandig Art

Renowned for vibrant ethnic paintings depicting nature and ancestral spirits. Talaandig art has gained international recognition for its expressive storytelling.

🎵

Indigenous Music

Traditional instruments like the kulintang, agong, and gimbal create the soulful soundscapes of Talaandig ceremonies and celebrations.

💃

Ritual Dances

Sacred and festive dances are performed during harvest seasons and tribal ceremonies, each step carrying deep spiritual and cultural meaning.

🌾

Kaamulan Festival

Impasug-ong participates in Bukidnon's famous Kaamulan Festival — a grand celebration of the 7 indigenous tribes of the province held every February.
